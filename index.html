Brand Identity System

Visual standards, typography, color, and usage guidelines.

Brand Overview

Gilbert Harrell is a distinguished Georgia law firm founded in 1871. The visual identity communicates authority, tradition, and trust through a rich navy palette, warm gold accents, and an elegant serif/sans-serif type pairing.

"Confidential. Strategic. Proven."

Imagery Style

Photography is central to the brand. Always pair with dark overlays when text is placed over images. Avoid staged corporate stock.

Regional & local — coastal Georgia, aerial
Professional & warm — library, boardroom
Atmospheric — golden-hour, warm amber tones

Do: Regional & local (aerial coastal Georgia, Brunswick waterways, historic downtown streetscapes)  ·  Professional & warm (attorneys in natural settings)  ·  Atmospheric (golden-hour light, dusk skies, warm amber tones)
Don't: Staged corporate stock  ·  Cool/blue-toned imagery  ·  Generic city skylines  ·  Text over images without dark overlay

Voice & Tone

Link to full Voice and Tone Doc

Every piece of copy should feel like it comes from a trusted, long-established firm — not a corporate agency. Formal, but never cold.

Brand Voice Attributes

  • Authoritative and established ("Since 1871")
  • Confident and direct ("CONFIDENTIAL. STRATEGIC. PROVEN.")
  • Client-focused and relationship-driven
  • Formal but approachable
  • No jargon-heavy language

Writing in Practice

  • Lead with client outcomes, not firm credentials
  • Short, declarative sentences for headlines
  • Use "we" and "our team" — avoid impersonal language
  • Reference Georgia and regional identity where natural
  • Avoid passive voice in CTAs and headings

"They are an integral part of our team. Their guidance has been invaluable at every stage of our growth."

Example — Client Testimonial (relationship-driven, authentic)
